General

Q: Are Territory tiles discarded back to the pool?
A: Just to a discard pile. If you run out of the main pool of tiles, you can shuffle the discards back in.

Q: Can benefits be taken in any order?
A: Yes, unless otherwise specified.

Q: What is the timing of gaining Landmarks (before or after benefits/bonuses)?
A: First! Before benefits/bonuses

Q: After Dictatorship tech card is played, are mandatory advancements no longer mandatory, or do they override the Dicatorship's lock? Examples are the Renaissance, Socialism, Age of Discovery, and Dark Ages tech cards.
A: Dictatorship prevents all movement on a track until that player's next turn. If it's played first, it prevents token movements from other cards.
Socialism: You could not choose the blocked track.
Renaissance: Advance on all tracks except the blocked track.
Age of Discovery: If the blocked track is rolled, only the person who played the Dictatorship tapestry advances (they're the only one not blocked on that track).
Dark Ages: You must choose to regress on the blocked track and two others (you're blocked from advancing, not regressing). Take the advancement on the remaining track.

Q: When aligning terrain for points during exploration, what's considered an aesthetic river, and what isn't?
A: The thin water spaces between territories are considered aesthetic. They are there so tiles look nice when lining up against water spaces. However, anything thicker represents water, which is not aligned. Re: Tile terrain question (does this tile score?)

Tracks

Exploration

1. SCOUTING - Gain 2 territory tiles.
2. RAFTS - Explore: Place 1 territory tile from your supply on the map, gain 1 VP per aligning side, and gain the benefit on the tile. [BONUS: Explore: Place 1 territory tile from your supply on the map, gain 1 VP per aligning side, and gain the benefit on the tile.]
3. WAGONS - Explore OR gain 1 farm.
4. NAVIGATION - Gain 1 territory tile, then explore. [LANDMARK: Lighthouse]
5. SHIPS - Gain 1 VP for each territory you control. [BONUS: Gain 1 VP for each territory you control.]
6. TUNNELS - Gain 1 territory tile and 1 farm. [BONUS: Gain 1 territory tile and 1 farm.]
7. TRAINS - Gain 2 territory tiles, then explore. [LANDMARK: Train Station]
8. CARS - Gain 1 farm, then gain 1 VP for each farm in your capital city. [BONUS: Gain 1 farm, then gain 1 VP for each farm in your capital city.]
9. AIRPLANES - Gain 2 territory tiles, then explore anywhere on the map. [BONUS: Gain 2 territory tiles, then explore anywhere on the map.]
10. SPACE SHUTTLE - Gain 1 VP per technology track space you’ve advanced. [BONUS: Gain 1 VP per technology track space you’ve advanced.] [LANDMARK: Launch Pad]
11. INTERSTELLAR TRAVEL - Gain 3 space tiles, then explore 1 of them (place explored space tiles next to your income mat).
12. WARPGATES - Explore a space tile from your supply. [BONUS: Explore a space tile from your supply]

Science

1. ASTRONOMY - Research: Roll the science die to advance for free (don’t gain benefit & bonus).
2. MATHEMATICS - Gain 1 tapestry card. [BONUS: Gain 1 tapestry card.]
3. HERBALISM - Research (don’t gain benefit & bonus) OR gain 1 house.
4. MEDICINE - Gain 1 VP for each tech card in your supply; also gain 1 tapestry card. [LANDMARK: Apothecary]
5. CHEMISTRY - Research to gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any). [BONUS: Research to gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any).]
6. BIOLOGY - Research to gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any) OR gain 1 house.
7. ACADEMIC RESEARCH - Gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any) of your current position on any advancement track. [LANDMARK: Academy]
8. NUTRITION - Gain 1 house, then gain 1 VP for each house in your capital city.
9. PHYSICS - Advance on 1 of these tracks, then gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any).
10. NEUROSCIENCE - Regress on 1 of these tracks, then gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any). [LANDMARK: Laboratory]
11. QUANTUM PHYSICS - Advance on 1 of these tracks, then gain the benefit & pay to gain the bonus (if any). Then do it again (same or different track).
12. ALIEN BIOLOGY - Roll 4 science dice to advance (don’t gain the benefits & bonuses). Gain 5 VP per die that would push you off a track.

3. STONE TOOLS - Invent 1 tech card OR gain 1 market.
4. METALLURGY - You may discard all 3 face-up tech cards and replace them. Invent 1 tech card. [LANDMARK: Forge]
5. GLASS - Gain either a farm, house, or armory. [BONUS: Gain either a farm, house, or armory.]
6. STEEL - Gain 1 VP for each armory in your capital city and gain 1 market. [BONUS: Gain 1 VP for each armory in your capital city and gain 1 market.]
7. RUBBER - You may discard all 3 face-up tech cards and replace them. Invent 2 tech cards (one at a time). [LANDMARK: Rubber Works]
8. PLASTIC - Gain 1 market, then gain 1 VP for each market in your capital city. [BONUS: Gain 1 market, then gain 1 VP for each market in your capital city.]
9. ELECTRONICS - In any order, upgrade 1 tech card and gain the circle benefit of 1 tech card in your middle row.
10. COMPUTERS - Gain 1 VP per military and science track space you’ve advanced. [LANDMARK: Tech Hub]
11. NANOTECHNOLOGY - In any order, upgrade 1 tech card & gain the square benefit of 1 tech card in your top row. [BONUS: In any order, upgrade 1 tech card & gain the square benefit of 1 tech card in your top row.]
12. AI SINGULARITY - Remove your player token from the technology track and place it on the starting space of any track. Gain 1 of each resource. Military

1. ARCHERY - Conquer: Place an outpost on a territory adjacent to a territory you control. Roll the 2 conquer dice and pick 1 of the benefits rolled.
2. BLADED WEAPONS - Gain 1 tapestry card. [BONUS: Gain 1 tapestry card.]
3. WALLS - Conquer 1 territory OR gain 1 armory.
4. STANDING ARMY - Gain 1 worker and gain 1 VP per territory tile in your supply. [LANDMARK: Barracks]
5. CAVALRY - Conquer 1 territory and gain 1 armory.
6. GUNPOWDER - Conquer 1 territory and gain 1 tapestry card. [BONUS: Conquer 1 territory and gain 1 tapestry card.]
7. TANKS - Conquer 1 territory. If that territory was controlled by an opponent, gain the benefits of both conquer dice. [LANDMARK: Tank Factory]
8. WARPLANES - Conquer 1 territory anywhere on the map. [BONUS: Conquer 1 territory anywhere on the map.]
9. ANTI-AIRCRAFT DEFENSE - Gain 1 armory and gain 1 VP per tapestry card (in hand and on your income mat). Rulings: Covered tapestries also count
10. NUCLEAR BOMB - Gain 1 VP per exploration track space you’ve advanced. Also play a tapestry on top of your current tapestry. Only the new card is active. [LANDMARK: Fusion Reactor]
11. DRONE ASSASSINS - Score your capital city. [BONUS: Score your capital city.]
12. MECHS - Conquer 1 territory (gain the benefits of both conquer dice). Also gain a random additional civilization.

Architects

Text: The Architects value an organized and structured capital city.

Each time you complete a district, if all income buildings in it are identical, gain +1 [RESOURCE]. There must be at least 1 income building in that district.

Whenever you score your capital city (<<[CITY]>>), for each complete row/column where all the income buildings are identical (min. 1), that row/column is worth <<2>>, not <<1>>.

Civilization Adjustment: When starting the game with the Architects with 3 or more total players, gain 10 VP per opponent (don’t do this if you gain the Architects during the game).

Futurists

Text: The Futurists mysteriously began their civilization with significant advancements.

When you gain this civilization: Advance on each track by exactly 4 spaces (with the end of each track as the limit). Do not gain benefits, bonuses, or landmarks. Also, gain 1 of each resource.

Civilization Adjustment: When starting the game or gaining the Futurist’s starting benefits, lose 1 culture and any other 1 resource of your choice.

Inventors

Text: The Inventors start with 4 player tokens here. At the beginning of your income turns (2-5), you may move a player token from here to any tech card (yours or an opponent's). That card is instantly upgraded, and it ignores top-row prerequisites. If it is an opponent's tech card, you may gain the benefit after they do (if it's your card, gain the benefit as normal).

Whenever an opponent's tech card with your token on it is upgraded to the top row, the opponent gains the benefit and then you gain the tech card, placing it on your bottom row. Your token remains on it. Landmarks on tech cards already gained by the opponent do nothing for you.

Rulings:
Q: Are the top-row prerequisites ignored for the rest of the game?
A: Yes

Nomads

Text: The Nomads prefer to expand their civilization outwards rather than focusing on their capital city.

Whenever you gain a building or landmark, you may place it either in your capital city or on the map in 1 or 2 locations:

  • On a territory you control containing exactly 1 other token (outpost or building). If you do, gain [RESOURCE]. Opponents can't conquer these territories.
  • On an empty territory adjacent to one you control. This gives you control of that territory, but it is not a conquer action. It is considered an outpost if an opponent conquers it.

You may still gain <<>> for buildings (i.e., <<HOUSE>>) whether they're on the map or in your capital city.

Traders

Text: The Traders prefer to connect with other civilizations economically, not militarily. Start with 4 player tokens here.

At the beginning of your income turns (2-5), you may place a player token from here on any territory on the map matching one of these descriptions:

  • Empty territory: Gain <<1>> per adjacent territory controlled by opponents. If an opponent later conquers this territory, you may gain the benefit of any unselected conquer die as if you rolled it.
  • Territory with exactly 1 opponent outpost token and nothing else: Gain the benefit on the territory (if any).

Remember that a territory with 2 tokens (of any type) on it cannot be conquered.

Civilization Adjustment: When starting the game with the Traders, gain any 2 resources (don't do this if you gain the Traders during the game).
